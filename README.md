# softball-fraud-check-pages
D1 Softball Early-Season Volatility Checker (Feb 2026)

# ⚡ Executive Summary
This dashboard identifies early-season volatility, regression candidates, and structurally strong contenders in NCAA Division I softball using Pythagorean expectation, strength of schedule, and proprietary team strength metrics.
It separates:

- Teams outperforming their run differential (“running hot”)
- Teams underperforming their underlying metrics (“quiet risers”)
- Teams whose record is validated by strength of schedule

By combining Fraud Delta (Actual Win% − Pythagorean Win%), SOS, and TSR, the tool flags:

- Inflated records built on weak schedules
- Hidden strength masked by close losses
- True contenders battle-tested against quality opposition

This functions as a decision-support system for coaching prep, opponent scouting, and bracket forecasting.

<br>

## 🎯 Coaching Implications

### 📉 Regression Candidates (Overperforming + Weak SOS):
- Likely cooling once conference schedule tightens
- Record may not reflect sustainable run profile
- Game prep should focus on forcing neutral variance situations
- Emphasize bullpen exposure and late-inning pressure

### 📈 Late-Season Risers (Underperforming + High TSR):
- Underlying strength suggests positive correction
- Strong buy-low scouting targets
- Don’t underestimate based on record alone
- Tournament upside higher than surface resume

### 🏆 True Contenders (Stable + High SOS):
- Record validated by competition quality
- Less variance-driven
- Reliable postseason profile
- Lower regression risk

### 🔎 Practical Coaching Use:
- Identify which ranked teams are structurally inflated
- Prioritize scouting resources based on volatility tier
- Shape rotation strategy when facing high killshot teams
- Adjust tournament projections based on regression likelihood
